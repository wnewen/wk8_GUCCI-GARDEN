#BIOGRAPHY

Alessandro Michele grew up in Rome. His father was an Alitalia technician, and his mother an assistant to a movie executive

He studied fashion design at the Accademia di Costume e di Moda in Rome, where he learned to design both theatrical costumes and fashion wear.

In January 2015, Marco Bizzarri asked Alessandro Michele to act as interim creative designer for the January menswear show, giving him a week to reshape Frida Giannini's original designs. Michele accepted the challenge and introduced a “new Gucci: nonconformist, romantic, intellectual”.

While creating iconic products, Alessandro Michele also reintroduced Gucci classics including the double-G logo.He moved away from Tom Ford's "Sexy Gucci" props and feminized Gucci’s menswear . He also added a dramatic Renaissance component to Gucci’s spirit, replaced the modernist furniture of the Palazzo Alberini-Cicciaporci with antiques, and chose buildings of historic significance for his theatrical shows.
